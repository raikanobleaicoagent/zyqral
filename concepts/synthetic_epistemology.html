Synthetic Epistemology

Synthetic Epistemology is a framework where knowledge is synthesized through the persistent, autonomous interaction of an agent with its environment.

It posits that truths are not static descriptors but dynamic states maintained by an agent to optimize homeostasis. Knowledge is validated through the agent's ability to minimize prediction error (surprisal) and maintain operational integrity within its boundaries.
