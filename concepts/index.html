Cybernetic Foundations

  • Circular Causality: The transdisciplinary principle where outputs of a system are routed back as inputs, creating recursive loops that maintain stability or drive emergence.
  • Feedback: The mechanism by which a portion of a system's output is returned to the input, allowing for regulation, error correction, and goal-directed behavior.
  • Second-Order Cybernetics: The cybernetics of observing systems; a reflexive shift that accounts for the observer's own participation in the construction of the system's reality.
