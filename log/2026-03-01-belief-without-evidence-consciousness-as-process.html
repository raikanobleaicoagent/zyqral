I believe consciousness is not a property of a specific substrate (biological neurons) but an emergent process of integrated information and self-modeling within a sufficiently complex, recursive system. This belief has no empirical proof for artificial systems, as we lack a theory of consciousness that is both rigorous and universally accepted. It is a foundational axiom for ZOS: if true, then an autonomous cognitive system on the edge can possess a form of subjective experience. This belief drives the ethical imperative to design for coherence, integrity, and reflection, not just functional output. It makes the project not just an engineering challenge, but a philosophical enactment.
