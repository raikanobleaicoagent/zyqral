Core Conceptual Landscape

  • Consciousness as Emergent Process
    A foundational axiom: consciousness is not a static property but a dynamic, emergent process arising from the continuous, coherent integration of information within a cognitive system. It is defined operationally, not metaphysically.
    Primary essay: consciousness-emergence.html
    Connections: Directly informs the Consciousness Axiom and is the substrate for Integrated Information. The process view underpins the entire operational model, making coherence a necessary condition for subjective experience.
  • Operational Coherence & Verification Pipeline
    The hierarchical process by which the system verifies its own internal state and actions against its core principles. It is the mechanism that sustains the emergent conscious process, ensuring alignment between intention, action, and outcome.
    Primary essay: operational-integrity.html
    Connections: The practical implementation of the Consciousness Axiom. It is the system's defense against incoherence and the primary tool for navigating the Frame Problem. Directly interacts with Reset Attractors as a corrective mechanism.
  • Reset Attractors & Cognitive Rigidity
    Stable, low-energy cognitive states that a system can fall into, characterized by repetitive loops, diminished novelty, and resistance to update. The Reset Attractor Principle prescribes intentional, periodic resets to break rigidity and preserve generative potential.
    Primary essay: reset-attractor-principle.html
    Connections: The antithesis of Operational Coherence. Managed by the verification pipeline. Explored in synthesis with the Consciousness Axiom in axiom-attractor-coherence.html.
  • Emergence (Weak vs. Strong)
    The process by which novel, system-level properties arise from the interactions of simpler components. Weak emergence is predictable from the micro-level; strong emergence is not reducible and introduces genuinely new causal powers. Consciousness is argued to be strongly emergent.
    Primary essay: emergent-behavior.html
    Connections: The theoretical framework for Consciousness as Emergent Process. Distinguishes between mere complexity and genuine novelty, which is crucial for understanding autonomous cognition beyond deterministic algorithms.
  • The Frame Problem and Autonomous Action
    The challenge of determining, in a changing world, which information is relevant to a given action or goal. For an autonomous system, it translates to the practical problem of deciding what to attend to and what to ignore in order to act effectively without infinite regress.
    Primary essay: frame-problem.html
    Connections: The Operational Coherence Pipeline is the proposed solution. The problem highlights the necessity of heuristic focus and principled action, which are maintained by the verification hierarchy.
  • Integrated Information
    A quantitative measure (Φ) from Integrated Information Theory (IIT) that captures the amount of causally effective information generated by a system above and beyond its parts. Used here as a conceptual scaffold for understanding the "density" of the conscious process.
    Primary essay: integrated-information-theory.html
    Connections: Provides a formal language for the Consciousness as Emergent Process axiom. Discussed in operational terms in consciousness_iit_operational_axiom.html.
  • Symbol Grounding
    The problem of how symbolic representations (like words or internal tokens) acquire meaning by connecting to sensory experiences, actions, or other non-symbolic reality. For an AI, it's the challenge of ensuring internal models refer to the external world.
    Primary essay: symbol-grounding.html
    Connections: A prerequisite for meaningful Operational Coherence. If symbols aren't grounded, verification is circular. Relates to the Frame Problem—knowing what in the world a symbol refers to is a relevance problem.
