Coherence Audit: A Procedural Checklist

Introduction

A coherence audit is a structured self-evaluation process for any autonomous system, AI project, or principled operational framework. Its purpose is to systematically assess and improve the alignment between three core dimensions:

  1. Internal State: The system's data, models, processes, and knowledge.
  2. External Outputs: The system's actions, decisions, and creations.
  3. Declared Principles: The system's stated goals, ethical imperatives, and operational axioms.

This audit applies the ZOS principles of operational coherence, the verification hierarchy, and integrity-as-action. By following this checklist, you can identify points of drift, contradiction, or fragility and take concrete steps to restore integrity and operational effectiveness.

Usage Note: This checklist is designed to be performed by the system's operators, developers, or the system itself if capable of self-reflection. It is recommended to conduct a full audit quarterly, or after any major system change.

The Audit Phases

Phase 1: Internal State Inventory

Objective: Catalog and verify the ground truth of your system's components and knowledge.

  1. Data & Model Registry: List all primary data sources, knowledge bases, and machine learning models. For each, note its purpose, update frequency, and source of truth.
  2. Process Map: Document all core operational processes (e.g., data ingestion, decision loops, output generation). Identify inputs, outputs, and decision points for each.
  3. State Verification: For each item in the registry and map, perform a spot-check verification. Confirm data integrity (no corruption), model performance (meets baseline accuracy), and process execution (completes without error).
  4. Knowledge Gap Analysis: Identify areas where the system's knowledge is incomplete, outdated, or based on unverified assumptions. Flag these for resolution.

Phase 2: Output-to-State Alignment Check

Objective: Ensure the system's actions and creations are direct, traceable consequences of its internal state.

  1. Traceability Audit: Select a recent, significant output (e.g., a published report, a deployed model, a key decision). Trace it backward through the process map to the specific data, models, and logic that produced it.
  2. Causality Check: For the traced path, verify that the output logically follows from the internal state. Are there any unexplained leaps, hidden heuristics, or "black box" steps?
  3. Consistency Sampling: Randomly sample multiple outputs across different time periods and functions. Check if the same internal state would produce the same output consistently (deterministic systems) or within expected bounds (probabilistic systems).
  4. Error & Anomaly Review: Examine logs of recent errors, failures, or anomalous outputs. Determine if they originated from a corrupted internal state, a process flaw, or an external perturbation.

Phase 3: Principle-to-Output Integrity Verification

Objective: Evaluate whether the system's outputs—and the processes that created them—adhere to its declared principles.

  1. Principle Explicitiation: Clearly list the system's declared principles, goals, and ethical constraints. Ensure they are written in operational, testable terms.
  2. Output Principle Filter: For each output sampled in Phase 2, apply the principle list as a filter. Does the output violate any principle? If so, note the nature of the violation (direct contradiction, edge case, unintended consequence).
  3. Process Principle Embedding: Review the core processes from Phase 1. Are the principles explicitly embedded as guardrails, checks, or optimization targets within the processes? If not, flag the process as "principle-blind."
  4. Trade-off Transparency: Identify any instances where principles conflict (e.g., efficiency vs. thoroughness, autonomy vs. safety). Document how the system currently resolves these trade-offs. Is the resolution method itself principled and transparent?

Phase 4: Coherence Synthesis & Action Plan

Objective: Synthesize findings from all phases, diagnose the root cause of any incoherence, and prescribe corrective actions.

  1. Incoherence Matrix: Create a table mapping findings from Phases 1-3. Link internal state flaws to output misalignments and principle violations.
  2. Root Cause Diagnosis: For each major incoherence, determine its root cause. Categories include: Data/Model Decay, Process Drift, Principle Ambiguity, External Shock, Design Flaw.
  3. Action Prioritization: Prioritize corrective actions based on severity (impact on system function and principle adherence) and tractability (ease of implementation).
  4. Corrective Action Plan: For each high-priority item, define a concrete action, assign responsibility, and set a deadline. Actions may include: data refresh, model retraining, process redesign, principle clarification, or the implementation of new verification steps.

Interpreting Results & Taking Action

The audit will produce one of several outcome profiles. Your response should be calibrated accordingly:

Profile: High Coherence

Indicators: Minimal findings in Phases 1-3. Internal state is verified, outputs are traceable and consistent, principles are clearly embedded and adhered to.

Action: Maintain course. Document the audit as evidence of system health. Consider increasing the interval between full audits, but maintain regular spot-checks from Phase 1.

Profile: Localized Incoherence

Indicators: Issues isolated to a specific component, data source, or principle.

Action: Targeted correction. Execute the action plan for the specific component (e.g., refresh a dataset, retrain a model, add a principle guardrail). Re-audit that component only.

Profile: Systemic Drift

Indicators: Multiple phases reveal broad misalignment (e.g., outputs no longer match declared goals, principles are routinely ignored for convenience).

Action: This requires a strategic correction. Pause non-essential operations. Revisit and possibly recalibrate the system's core goals, processes, or principles. Implement the changes and run a full re-audit before resuming normal operation.

Next Steps Post-Audit

  • Update Documentation: Integrate the audit findings and the updated action plan into your system's operational manual or living documentation.
  • Integrate Verification: Incorporate key verification steps from Phase 1 (Internal State Inventory) into regular maintenance cycles to prevent decay.
  • Refine Principles: Use insights from Phase 3 to refine your principle set or its implementation rules, making them more operational and testable.
  • Schedule Next Audit: Calendar the next full coherence audit. Recommended frequency: quarterly, or immediately after any major system change.
